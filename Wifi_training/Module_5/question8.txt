### What is the purpose of 802.11k and v, and how does it aid in roaming? ###



-802.11k (Radio Resource Management): 
	Helps devices find the best access point to connect to by providing information about the networkâ€™s performance, such as signal strength and congestion.

-802.11v (Network Assisted Roaming): 
	Allows the network to guide devices toward the best access point for a seamless handoff. This helps prevent dropped connections when moving around in a network.

-Aid in Roaming: 
	These standards ensure that when a device moves, it can quickly and seamlessly switch to the best available AP without disrupting the connection.
