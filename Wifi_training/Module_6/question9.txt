### What problem does 802.1X solve in a network? ###



-802.1X solves the problem of unauthorized access to network resources, especially in enterprise environments. 
-It provides port-based Network Access Control (PNAC), ensuring that only authenticated users or devices can access the network.


-Key problems it addresses include:

	-Preventing unauthorized users from joining wired or wireless networks.
	-Allowing centralized authentication, typically using a RADIUS server.
	-Supporting per-user credentials and dynamic session keys, improving accountability and security.
	-Enforcing network policies before granting access, such as device compliance checks.

By using 802.1X, organizations can tightly control who and what connects to their network infrastructure.
